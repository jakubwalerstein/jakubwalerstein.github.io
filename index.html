  • samples and sound design

    • tape experiments

        The recordings labed (VHS tape) were created by recording sounds to VHS cassettes using a JVC DR-MV7 VCR, then physically manipulating the tape as it moved through the record and playback heads to create pitch warble, noise, and dropouts. Those labeled (cassette) include samples that were recorded to standard cassette tapes for subtle saturation and pitch warble.

    • ambience & textures

        The ambience & textures section consists of experiments in textural sound design. Tools used include analog synthesizers, heavily processed field recordings, and electric guitar.

    • yamaha CS10 feedback

        These are unprocessed recordings of a Yamaha CS10 monophonic analog synthesizer at the Apple Music Creation Apps Cupertino studio. The sounds here make use of a feedback loop created by routing the output of the CS10 back into its external input. The CS10 was played and recorded through a mic'd guitar amp.

    • drum kit recordings

        These drum beats and breaks are intended to demonstrate an effective drum microphone configuration. They were recorded with a Yamaha Stage Custom Birch drum kit in the IUPUI CLEAR Lab recording studio, using a 7-microphone Sennheiser evolution drum microphone kit. Outside of phase adjustment, there is no processing on the signal. For example, the snare was captured by using two microphones, one above pointing down at snare head and one below pointing up at the snare wires, and then inverting the phase of the signal from the lower microphone.

    • piano strings

        These sounds come from a recording session focused on a 5'7" Yamaha G2 baby grand piano in Carmel, IN. The most notable sounds in this pack are the sustains, which were created by playings an alto saxophone or a Jewish shofar into the body of the piano while the sustain pedal was held. This allowed the piano strings to resonate with the horn, creating an overtone-rich decay tail. These sounds were recorded using a mid-side microphone configuration.

    • digital synth loops 2018-2019

        These loops were created as a sample pack to be distributed to new members of the Purdue Music Production Club. They were created with digital synthesizer plug-ins over the span of the 2018-2019 academic year.
